Website Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to review whether a website is building trust, guiding action, and supporting conversion.

1. First impression
- Is the offer clear within the first screen?
- Does the homepage immediately explain who the business helps?
- Do visuals support credibility rather than distract from it?

2. Trust signals
- Are there testimonials, proof points, or client logos?
- Are contact details easy to find?
- Are team, process, or capability pages available?

3. Messaging clarity
- Is the language specific instead of generic?
- Are services explained in terms of outcomes?
- Is there a clear reason to choose this company now?

4. Structure and navigation
- Can users reach key pages in one or two clicks?
- Does the navigation reflect how buyers think?
- Is the mobile menu as clear as the desktop menu?

5. Conversion friction
- Is there a strong primary call to action?
- Are forms short, clear, and easy to complete?
- Is there a low-friction option like booking a call or requesting an audit?

6. Page quality
- Do service pages explain scope, deliverables, and fit?
- Do case studies show problem, solution, and result?
- Is pricing or engagement guidance available where appropriate?

7. Technical confidence
- Does the site load smoothly on mobile?
- Are there layout issues, overflow, or broken components?
- Are metadata, sitemap, and structured site pages in place?

8. Final review
- What feels unclear?
- What weakens trust?
- What would make it easier for the right client to act?
