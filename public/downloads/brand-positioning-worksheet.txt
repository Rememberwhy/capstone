CAPSTONE
Brand Positioning Worksheet

Use these prompts to clarify how your brand should be understood and remembered.

1. Audience
- Who are you trying to reach?
- What stage are they at when they arrive?
- What do they care about most when choosing a partner?

2. Problem
- What problem are you solving?
- What is frustrating, costly, or unclear for the client today?
- Why is that problem worth fixing now?

3. Offer
- What exactly do you provide?
- What is included?
- What kind of engagement is the best fit?

4. Differentiation
- Why should a client choose you over alternatives?
- What do you do with more rigor, taste, speed, or clarity?
- What do clients consistently mention after working with you?

5. Outcomes
- What changes after the work is complete?
- What improves in the business, not just in the design?
- What signs of success matter most?

6. Proof
- What results, testimonials, or examples support your claims?
- Which case studies best demonstrate your value?
- Where can you be more specific?

7. Brand tone
- Which three words should describe how the brand feels?
- What should the brand never sound like?
- What level of confidence or restraint should it carry?

8. Messaging draft
Complete the sentence:
"We help [audience] achieve [outcome] through [offer], with an approach built around [differentiator]."

Refine until it sounds specific, credible, and easy to repeat.
